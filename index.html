Old School: Film

Shooting with film forces you to slow down while shooting exposures. Once you run out of film, you have to purchase more. Imagine running out of space on an SD card, then having to buy a brand new one. You would be a lot more careful with your shooting.

Digital Single-Lens Reflex (DSLR)

DSLR cameras offer more versatility. They are easier for the beginner to start off on and build proficiency. If you take a bad exposure, you simply need to delete it from the memory. DSLR also offers more flexibility in post-production editing.
